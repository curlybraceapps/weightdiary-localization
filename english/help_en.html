Enter your weight each day and the app will estimate your weekly weight loss and track your progress towards your goal.

The yellow line is your trend line. Think of this as your average weight. Each day your weight will fluctuate up and down but this is mainly caused by changes in the amount of water and solids in your body.

The trend line cuts through the daily fluctuations and shows you what your underlying weight is doing. If most of your daily entries are below this line and the line is pointing downwards then these are signs you are losing weight.

If you get too many entries above the trend line then you may need to adjust your diet to get back on track.

These tell you at a glance if your weight on that day was above or below the trend line.

The current trend entry in the summary view shows how many days in a row you have been above or below the trend line.

The trend field shows your estimated rate of weight loss. It can be shown as a weekly loss/gain or as a daily calorie deficit/excess. Tap on the field to switch between the weekly and daily view.

The goal field shows your goal as a progress bar or a time estimate or how much weight you have left to lose. Tap on the field to switch between these views. Note: The time estimate can only be shown when you are losing weight.

Since trend and goal are calculated from your last few weight entries any changes to your diet may take a few days to show up fully.

The goal field is based on your current rate of weight loss as calculated from your last few weight entries. The goal calculator is a diet planning tool and it is your job to adjust your diet until the goal field matches your desired rate of weight loss.

We rely on your daily weigh-ins to calculate the trend line and estimate your weight loss. Missing the occasional day here and there won't hurt but if you miss too many days the estimates won't be as accurate.

Once a week is not enough to get accurate weight loss estimates and you won't be able to respond as quickly to changes in your rate of weight loss.

Our advice is trust the trend and just focus on keeping your daily entries below the trend line.

The default BMI scale goes from 18.5 (normal) on the left to 35 (obese) on the right. Remember, these are guidelines only and healthy BMI ranges can vary by country, age, height and other factors. The lower and upper BMI range can be customised in the settings if you have the paid version of our app.
